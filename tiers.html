Build Tiers

Pick a budget, not a different machine

All three tiers share the same canopy / reservoir / brain architecture, the same MQTT topic scheme, and the same rule-based policy engine. The differences are entirely in which parts sit behind that logic.

Full v1 — Honesty Check

$814 on the spreadsheet, ~$940 as actually ratified

Project_Eden_BOM.xlsx still reflects the pre-commercial-v1 design. hardware-design-v1.md §9 adds hardware the sheet hasn't caught up to yet — real parts, real cost, not yet a spreadsheet row.

Auto top-off pump/valve $20 · ultrasonic mister $20 · worm-gear lift motor $27.50 · limit switches $4 · DRV8871 H-bridge $6.50 · VL53L1X ToF $10 · leak sensor $5.50 · PCF8574 $4 · PCA9685 $6 · Mini-Fit Jr connectors $3 · 24V PSU/GFCI power delta $20 — $126.50 not yet on the sheet. $813.85 + $126.50 ≈ $940, the real ratified number. Anyone budgeting off the spreadsheet alone is undercounting Full v1 by about that much.

One more layer of honesty: everything on this page runs on the design spreadsheet's planning prices, so the three tiers stay comparable to each other. The Bill of Materials page prices the same Full v1 design against live July-2026 vendor listings and lands at $1,133.94 for canopy + reservoir + brain + power/frame (its scope excludes the grow-site subsystem). The delta is real market movement, not new scope — concentrated in the Raspberry Pi 5 8GB ($80 → $175, an industry-wide DRAM shortage) and the SCD40 CO2 sensor ($19.95 → $44.95). At live prices the cuts below get bigger, not smaller: the Trimmed Primary's SCD40 cut is worth $44.95 on today's listings, and the Budget tier's owner-supplied brain sidesteps the single most shortage-inflated subsystem on the live sheet ($256.99 for the Pi 5 kit).

Trimmed Primary — $754

Same machine, four cuts, all telemetry

Every cut here is hardware with no consumer in canopy-core or reservoir-core today — checked against the actual control code, not assumed.

−$19.95 — SCD40 CO2 sensorThe design doc calls CO2 "monitoring only, v2" — no CO2 actuator exists anywhere in the system. Re-add path: drop-in I2C part on the existing sensor bus once a v2 CO2 policy ships.

−$20 — Pi 5 8GB → 4GBThe 8GB headroom is for a vision pipeline that's currently a stub. Re-add: swap the Pi when real inference lands.

−$15 — Defer 2nd canopy fanThe PCB header stays populated-ready; add the fan if one fan can't overcome carbon-filter back-pressure.

−$5 — Unused grow-site DS18B20Already optional, feeds nothing.

Optional further cut, not recommended: AS7341 → BH1750 lux sensor, −$16 → $738. v1.1 re-scopes the AS7341 to down-facing plant-health reflectance, which serves the max-automation goal — cutting it trades away a near-term capability for a one-time saving.

What you actually lose vs. Full v1: CO2 logging (not control — there is none), Pi RAM headroom, and one fan of redundancy. Every control loop, every safety interlock, every sensor those loops consume, and the camera are identical.
Budget — ≈$560

Cheapest build that still grows anything, fully automated

Designed under four constraints: the camera stays, grow capability is never downgraded (any hydroponic crop, maximum harvest/quality/automation), real pH/EC stays, and the brain moves to a computer you already run.

Canopy

$160
  • Seeed XIAO ESP32-S3 Sense ($14) replaces the WROOM module + OV2640 + fabricated 4-layer PCB + passives (~$64 together) — keeps the camera and PSRAM
  • SHT40 kept
  • BH1750 lux sensor instead of AS7341
  • No SCD40
  • One fan + MOSFET/0–10V dimmer breakout modules instead of a fabricated board
  • HLG 65 V2 kept at $99

Reservoir

$155
  • The reservoir is the product — essentially untouched
  • ADS1115, DFRobot pH + EC, DS18B20 all stay
  • Circulation pump, 3× dosing pumps, air pump, dual floats all stay
  • Only the flow sensor goes (−$6, telemetry-only)

Grow Site

$83
  • 3" PVC grid, LECA, uniseals all kept — the any-crop requirement
  • Only the unused regional temp probe cut

Tent + Shell

$161
  • Real tent, carbon filter, GFCI/fused power all kept
  • Downgrades: a relay board instead of the switching board, a 120mm circulation fan

Brain

$0
  • Owner's existing computer runs the Mosquitto/InfluxDB/Grafana/policy stack
  • Honest caveat: v1's design has the brain hosting its own Wi-Fi AP for module pairing. On an owned computer, that machine either hosts the AP itself or pairing moves to the home LAN — a small open design question (D7), flagged, not hidden.

Tradeoff vs. Full v1: loses the fabricated PCB (breakout-board wiring instead — more assembly labor, less polish, same circuits), spectral reflectance sensing (lux only), CO2 logging, flow telemetry, the 2nd fan, and the dedicated always-on Pi appliance. Keeps every control loop, every safety interlock, the camera, the full dosing stack, and the identical grow path (light, 3" sites, LECA).

Why Not $450

The next $110 only comes out of the grow path

Below $560, the remaining savings stop being telemetry or polish and start being the plants themselves: a $60 LM301-class light board instead of the HLG 65 (−$39), 2" PVC + rockwool instead of 3" PVC + LECA (−$31, locks out large fruiting crops), no carbon filter (−$20, no odor control), and a camera-less MCU (−$8) — together, ≈$460. That's a leafy-greens-only machine, and it isn't what Project Eden sells. Deliberately not offered.

Never-Cut Floor

What no tier ever touches

Aeration & circulationRDWC roots die in hours without dissolved oxygen — the air pump and circulation pump are exempt from every interlock, in every tier.

Lab-grade pH/ECBad chemistry data means dosing the wrong amount of the wrong thing — never downgraded to a cheaper analog proxy.

SHT40-class RH/tempVPD is the master control variable every tier's fan/mister PID runs against.

Dual float switchesHigh/low dry-run protection for every pump in the reservoir.

GFCI + fusingThe one place every tier still touches mains.

0–10V dimmable quality lightThe HLG 65 V2 is the yield driver — every tier keeps it.

False economies rejected by design
