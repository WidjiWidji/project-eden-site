System Architecture

Power in one place, intelligence at the edge

Every mains connection in the tent lives in a single Power Entry Box, behind one GFCI. Everything downstream of it runs on 24V DC, carried through raceways built into the PVC frame.

The Power Entry Box is the only place in the system that touches wall power: a GFCI feeds a certified 24V PSU, a Pi PSU, and the light driver. The 24V bus threads through the frame to the Canopy (ESP32-S3) and Reservoir (ESP32-C3) modules. The Brain — a Raspberry Pi 5 — hosts its own Wi-Fi access point; modules ship pre-paired to it, and MQTT stays entirely local (eden/<tent-id>/<module>/<channel>). An uplink to the home LAN or cloud is optional. Camera frames reach the Brain as JPEGs over HTTP.

Modules

Three modules, one job each

Canopy watches and shapes the light and air above the plants. Reservoir owns the water. Brain decides.

Canopy

ESP32-S3
  • Motorized light lift — worm-gear, self-locking, hard limit switches, stall cutoff. AI holds optimal light distance as plants grow.
  • 2→4 PWM fans, two 0–10V dimming channels
  • 24V ultrasonic mister
  • Aspirated sensor duct — SHT40 + SCD40 satellite board, pulled clear of light and PCB heat for meteorology-grade air readings
  • VL53L1X ToF — canopy distance, modeled into PPFD and plant-height growth telemetry
  • AS7341 down-facing plant-health reflectance
  • OV2640 camera
  • Spare STEMMA-QT expansion jack

Reservoir

ESP32-C3
  • pH, EC, and water-temp probes
  • Three dosing pumps
  • Auto top-off — doubles as EC dilution
  • Leak sensor
  • Float switches and a flow sensor
  • Circulation and the air pump are never interlocked — RDWC roots need continuous oxygenation, so only dosing halts on a fault.

Brain

Pi 5
  • Site Wi-Fi access point
  • MQTT broker
  • Policy engine — rule-based in v1, learned later
  • Web app (PWA)
  • Grafana
  • Battery-backed RTC
  • OTA image server
Control Philosophy

Three tiers, one rule

→ the fast loop never crosses the network
01

Edge reflexes

On-module control, no network dependency. Runs even if the Brain is gone.

02

Brain setpoints

Delivered over MQTT, bounded by a TTL — a stale setpoint expires back to edge defaults.

03

Cloud (optional)

Advisory only, reachable through the Brain's optional uplink.

P1
Stale sensor data
→ failsafe fan, hold light
P2
Over-temp
→ vent + dim + mister off
P3
Leak
→ halt dosing + top-off, never circulation
P4
Any fault
→ lift holds position

Two-sided VPD control

The fan pulls humidity down; the mister pushes it up. A signed PID with a deadband drives whichever direction is needed, and the two outputs are mutually exclusive — never fighting each other over the same air.

Dosing order

Settle-lockout between doses, EC corrected before pH, and top-off does double duty as EC dilution rather than a separate step.

Safety priority ladder

Checked in strict order every control cycle — a higher-priority condition always overrides a lower one, regardless of what the policy engine last asked for.

Safety by Design

Fails safe, recovers on its own

Single mains locationEvery wall-power connection sits behind one GFCI in the Power Entry Box — nowhere else in the tent touches mains.

≤24V everywhere elseNothing on the Canopy or Reservoir module, or the DC bus feeding them, exceeds 24V.

Self-locking liftWorm-gear drive holds position with power off — no drift, no drop.

Flood, dose, and mist budgetsHard caps on volume and runtime bound the worst case for every wet actuator.

Watchdogs + safe-state bootOn any reset, circulation and the air pump come up on; everything else stays off until the control loop is healthy.

Plug-yank recoveryPulling power and restoring it is a tested acceptance criterion, not an assumption.
