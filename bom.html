Pooled Cost

Where per-unit cost drops at pilot scale

These parts repeat across modules — buying them by the piece, once per unit, is the most inflated pricing in this BOM. At Wave B (10–50 units, per the evolution ladder on the Vision page), the same part numbers move to reel/bulk pricing.

Connector families

Molex Mini-Fit Jr (module power), JST-XH (actuators), and JST-SH/STEMMA-QT (sensors) all repeat across canopy, reservoir, and the power spine. Single-set pricing here (as low as $0.27/housing on DigiKey, $0.95/cable on Adafruit) drops further on reel buys once a pilot run justifies ordering by the hundred instead of the piece.

  • Mini-Fit Jr 2-pin — canopy + reservoir power drops
  • JST-XH 4-pin — fan/pump/actuator headers, both modules
  • JST-SH/STEMMA-QT — sensor bus, canopy + reservoir

Fasteners & print hardware

M3 hardware (screws, heat-set inserts, standoffs) repeats across all four printed enclosures — canopy, reservoir, brain, and the power entry box. Hardware-store single-pack pricing is inflated relative to a 100-count bulk pack, which runs pennies rather than dollars per piece.

Hookup wire & harness stock

24V bus wire and JST pigtail leads are bought as pre-cut lengths today, priced per-project. At pilot scale, bulk spooled stock plus a harness-house quote (the connector families' Evolution note in hardware-design-v1.md §2.3) replaces hand-cut, hand-crimped runs on the same connector families — lower marginal cost per harness, not a redesign.

Common passives/ICs — future reservoir PCB

Canopy's board already carries IRLZ44N MOSFETs, AMS1117/LP2950 LDOs, and TVS protection. hardware-design-v1.md §4.3/§11 confirms reservoir gets its first PCB spin at pilot volume, on the same 24V rail — likely sharing several of these exact part numbers. Reel pricing (100+ units) on parts shown above at single-unit DigiKey price (e.g. IRLZ44N at $2.10 each) is typically well under $1/unit.

Pricing Honesty

Why this total moved, and what's still open

Every price is a live listingEvery row above links to a real, dated vendor page fetched for this snapshot — DigiKey, Mouser, Adafruit, SparkFun, DFRobot, or a named reseller. Where a live page wouldn't yield a price (several Amazon listings block automated fetches), the line is marked (est.) and still links to the real listing, not an invented one.

Two things moved the total since our last estimateAn earlier internal planning figure (BOM_Tradeoffs.md, June 2026) put a mid-range build at ~$814. This page totals ~$1,134 — the delta is genuine new scope (rev B's lift, mister, ToF sensor, and GPIO/PWM expanders) plus real 2026 market movement on two parts: the Raspberry Pi 5 8GB (~$80 → $175–200, an industry-wide DRAM shortage, not Eden-specific) and the SCD40 CO₂ sensor (~$20 → $44.95).

What's still openThe HLG 65 V2 light's dimming behavior reads inconsistently across two retailers — one describes it non-dimmable out of the box, which would conflict with the canopy board's 0–10V op-amp dimmer circuit. That needs a bench check, not a spec-sheet read. A handful of other lines (the JLCPCB batch quote, several Amazon-only parts) need a live checkout to pin an exact number.

Pricing snapshot: July 2026, single-unit distributor/retail pricing (DigiKey, Mouser, Adafruit, SparkFun, DFRobot, named Amazon/reseller listings) — not bulk or pilot-volume quotes.
